The AFC originally started out as the American Football League (AFL) which was a seperate football league in the 1960s. It merged with the NFL and became the American Football Conference (AFC) in the year 1970. Three teams from the existing NFL were placed into this new conference: the Baltimore Colts, the Cleveland Browns, and the Pittsburgh Steelers.

The Kansas City Chiefs are the reigning AFC Champions as well as Super Bowl Champions. Their 2019 win was the first for the team since 1969.
